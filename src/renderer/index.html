How Your App Works as a Server:

  • ‚úÖ On Your Network: Anyone on your WiFi can join using your local IP
  • üîß From Internet: You need to configure port forwarding on your router
  • üñ•Ô∏è Your PC = Server: When you create a room, your computer hosts it
  • üîÑ Direct Connection: No middleman servers - direct peer-to-peer

üîß Port Forwarding Setup (5-10 minutes)

1
Access Your Router Settings

Open your browser and go to your router's admin page:

Login credentials are usually on your router's sticker

2
Find Port Forwarding Section

Look for one of these in your router settings:

  • "Port Forwarding"
  • "Virtual Servers"
  • "NAT" or "NAT Forwarding"
  • "Applications & Gaming"
3
Add Port Forwarding Rule
Service Name: SyncStream
Protocol: TCP
External Port: 5000
Internal Port: 5000
Internal IP: Your PC's IP
4
Save and Test

After saving, share this URL with friends:

http://YOUR_PUBLIC_IP:5000
üìö Router-Specific Guides:

‚úÖ Advantages of Port Forwarding

  • Direct Connection: No middleman servers - lowest latency
  • Full Control: Your data stays on your computer
  • No Limits: No bandwidth or user restrictions
  • Free Forever: No monthly fees or subscriptions
  • Best Performance: Direct peer-to-peer connection
  • Privacy: You control who can access your server

‚ö†Ô∏è Considerations

  • Setup Required: One-time 5-10 minute configuration
  • PC Must Stay On: Your computer hosts the room
  • IP May Change: Dynamic IPs require occasional updates
  • Security: Opens a port on your network (safe if configured correctly)
  • ISP Restrictions: Some ISPs block certain ports
